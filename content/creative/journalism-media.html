JOURNALISM & MEDIA

OVERVIEW

What is Journalism & Media?

Journalism and media involve gathering, analyzing, and presenting news and information to the public through various channels, including print, digital, television, and radio. In Australia, journalism and media are dynamic fields with opportunities in news organizations, broadcasting, digital media, and content creation.

What Will You Do?

  • Research and report on news stories, events, and trends
  • Write, edit, and publish articles, blogs, and multimedia content
  • Conduct interviews and investigations
  • Work with photographers, videographers, and editors to produce content
  • Use social media to engage audiences and promote stories
  • Stay updated on media laws, ethics, and industry trends

Salary Range in Australia

Entry-Level (Junior Journalist, 0-2 years): AUD $50,000 - $70,000 per year
Mid-Level (Journalist/Reporter, 3-6 years): AUD $70,000 - $90,000 per year
Senior-Level (Senior Journalist/Editor, 7+ years): AUD $90,000 - $120,000+ per year
Advanced Roles (Editor-in-Chief, Media Manager, 10+ years): AUD $120,000 - $150,000+ per year

2. Steps to Enter the Journalism & Media Industry in Australia

Step 1: Get Educated (University or Online Courses)

  • If you're in university, focus on journalism, media studies, and digital storytelling.
  • If you're self-learning, complete online courses and build a portfolio.

Step 2: Build a Portfolio

  • Write articles, blogs, or news stories for personal projects or student publications.
  • Create multimedia content (videos, podcasts, photo essays) to showcase your skills.
  • Publish your work on personal blogs, Medium, or LinkedIn.

Step 3: Gain Practical Experience

‚úÖ Internships & Work Placements

  • Apply for internships at news organizations, TV stations, or digital media companies.
  • Join university internship programs or industry partnerships.

‚úÖ Freelance & Side Projects

  • Work on freelance projects for local newspapers, blogs, or online platforms.
  • Contribute to community radio stations or podcasts.

‚úÖ Participate in Media Competitions

  • Join writing competitions (e.g., Walkley Awards for student journalists).
  • Participate in media hackathons or storytelling challenges.

Step 4: Create a Strong Resume & LinkedIn Profile

  • Highlight your writing, editing, and multimedia skills on your resume.
  • Include links to your portfolio, published work, or blog.
  • Australian employers value practical experience and a strong portfolio.

Step 5: Apply for Junior Roles

  • Apply via LinkedIn, Seek, Indeed, or media-specific job boards (e.g., Mediaweek).
  • Target companies like ABC, News Corp, Guardian Australia, and SBS.
  • Network with journalists and media professionals at industry events and LinkedIn.
