PUBLIC RELATIONS

OVERVIEW

What is Public Relations (PR)?

Public Relations (PR) is the practice of managing and maintaining the public image of individuals, organizations, or brands. PR professionals use strategic communication to build relationships with the public, media, and stakeholders. In Australia, PR is a growing field with opportunities in corporate communications, agencies, government, and non-profits.

What Will You Do?

  • Develop and execute PR strategies and campaigns
  • Write press releases, media pitches, and speeches
  • Build and maintain relationships with journalists, influencers, and stakeholders
  • Manage crisis communication and reputation management
  • Organize events, press conferences, and media interviews
  • Monitor media coverage and analyze the effectiveness of PR campaigns

Salary Range in Australia

Entry-Level (PR Assistant, 0-2 years): AUD $50,000 - $65,000 per year
Mid-Level (PR Manager, 3-6 years): AUD $65,000 - $90,000 per year
Senior-Level (Senior PR Manager, 7+ years): AUD $90,000 - $120,000+ per year
Advanced Roles (PR Director, 10+ years): AUD $120,000 - $150,000+ per year

2. Steps to Enter the Public Relations Industry in Australia

Step 1: Get Educated (University or Online Courses)

  • If you're in university, focus on public relations, media studies, and strategic communication.
  • If you're self-learning, complete online courses and build a portfolio.

Step 2: Build a Portfolio

  • Create mock PR campaigns for fictional or real brands.
  • Write press releases, media pitches, and social media content.
  • Showcase your work on LinkedIn, a personal website, or a portfolio platform.

Step 3: Gain Practical Experience

‚úÖ Internships & Work Placements

  • Apply for internships at PR agencies, corporate communications teams, or non-profits.
  • Join university internship programs or industry partnerships.

‚úÖ Freelance & Side Projects

  • Work on freelance PR projects for small businesses or startups.
  • Volunteer to manage communications for local organizations or events.

‚úÖ Participate in PR Competitions

  • Join PR case study competitions or industry awards (e.g., PRIA Awards).
  • Participate in networking events hosted by PR organizations.

Step 4: Create a Strong Resume & LinkedIn Profile

  • Highlight your PR skills, campaigns, and writing samples on your resume.
  • Include links to your portfolio, press releases, or published work.
  • Australian employers value practical experience and a strong portfolio.

Step 5: Apply for Entry-Level Roles

  • Apply via LinkedIn, Seek, Indeed, or PR-specific job boards (e.g., PRIA Careers).
  • Target companies like Edelman, Ogilvy, WE Communications, and corporate PR teams.
  • Network with PR professionals at industry events and LinkedIn.
