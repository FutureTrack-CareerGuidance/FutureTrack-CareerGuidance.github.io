CHEMICAL ENGINEERING

OVERVIEW

What is Chemical Engineering?

Chemical Engineering involves designing and optimizing chemical processes for industries such as pharmaceuticals, energy, mining, food production, and environmental sustainability. It integrates chemistry, physics, mathematics, and engineering principles to develop processes that convert raw materials into valuable products. In Australia, chemical engineers play a critical role in industries like mining and minerals processing, oil and gas, water treatment, renewable energy, and biotechnology. Major employers include BHP, Rio Tinto, Woodside, ExxonMobil, Orica, CSIRO, and government agencies.

What Will You Do?

  • Designing chemical plants and industrial processes
  • Developing sustainable energy solutions and carbon capture technologies
  • Ensuring safety and environmental compliance in manufacturing
  • Working with thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and materials science
  • Using process simulation software (Aspen HYSYS, MATLAB, ChemCAD, etc.)

Step 1: Study and Master Core Concepts

  • Learn process engineering, mass transfer, and fluid mechanics.
  • Gain experience with chemical plant design and process modeling.

Step 2: Get Hands-on Experience

  • Apply for internships in oil & gas, mining, or pharmaceuticals.
  • Work on university research projects in sustainable energy or biomaterials.

Step 3: Obtain Certification & Accreditation
