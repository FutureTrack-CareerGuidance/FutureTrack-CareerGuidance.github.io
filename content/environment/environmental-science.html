ENVIRONMENTAL SCIENCE & CONSERVATION

OVERVIEW

What is Environmental Science & Conservation?

Environmental Science & Conservation involves the study and protection of natural ecosystems, biodiversity, and environmental resources. This field focuses on addressing environmental challenges such as climate change, pollution, habitat destruction, and resource depletion. In Australia, environmental science and conservation are critical sectors, with opportunities in government agencies, non-profits, research institutions, and private companies.

What Will You Do?

  • Conduct research on environmental issues and ecosystems
  • Develop and implement conservation plans for wildlife and natural habitats
  • Monitor environmental quality (e.g., air, water, soil)
  • Advise on sustainable practices and environmental policies
  • Work on restoration projects for degraded ecosystems
  • Educate the public on environmental awareness and conservation

Salary Range in Australia

Entry-Level (Environmental Technician, 0-2 years): AUD $50,000 - $65,000 per year
Mid-Level (Environmental Scientist, 3-6 years): AUD $65,000 - $85,000 per year
Senior-Level (Senior Environmental Scientist, 7+ years): AUD $85,000 - $110,000+ per year
Advanced Roles (Environmental Manager/Conservation Director, 10+ years): AUD $110,000 - $140,000+ per year

Berlin is a hub for environmental innovation, with a strong focus on green urban development, circular economy initiatives, and climate-neutral projects. The German government's green transition policies are fueling investments in conservation.
