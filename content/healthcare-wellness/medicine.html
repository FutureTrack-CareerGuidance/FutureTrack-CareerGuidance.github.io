MEDICINE

OVERVIEW

What is Medicine?

Medicine is the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing diseases. It involves extensive study, practical training, and specialization. In Australia, medicine is a highly respected and regulated profession, with doctors playing a crucial role in the healthcare system. Australia has a world-class medical education system, with universities like the University of Melbourne, Monash University, and the University of Sydney offering top-tier medical programs.

What Will You Do?

  • Diagnosing and treating illnesses
  • Prescribing medications and therapies
  • Performing medical procedures and surgeries
  • Conducting medical research and clinical trials
  • Educating patients about health and disease prevention

2. Steps to Enter the Medical Industry in Australia

Step 1: Get into Medical School

  • Complete undergraduate or graduate entry pathways.
  • Maintain high academic performance (ATAR 99+ for undergraduate entry).
  • Pass UCAT (for undergraduate) or GAMSAT (for graduate entry).

Step 2: Complete a Medical Degree (MD)

  • Study theory (pre-clinical years) and gain hospital experience (clinical years).
  • Learn patient diagnosis, surgery, and healthcare ethics.
  • Participate in clinical rotations through various specialties.

Step 3: Internship (PGY1 - First Year as a Doctor)

‚úÖ Hospital Training

  • Work in a hospital for one year under supervision.
  • Gain experience in different medical departments (ER, surgery, general medicine, etc.).
  • Complete mandatory rotations required for registration.

Step 4: Residency (PGY2-3) - General Training

  • Work as a Resident Medical Officer (RMO) in a hospital.
  • Rotate through various medical specialties to find an area of interest.
  • Begin preparing applications for specialty training programs.

Step 5: Specialization Training (3-7 years, depending on specialty)

  • Apply for a medical specialty program (e.g., General Practice, Surgery, Psychiatry, Cardiology, etc.).
  • Complete registrar training under the supervision of senior doctors.
  • Pass fellowship exams to become a fully qualified specialist.

Step 6: Become a Consultant or GP (Final Stage)

  • After completing training, exams, and gaining experience, become a fully qualified doctor or specialist.
  • Work in public hospitals, private clinics, or start a private practice.
  • Continue professional development and education throughout your career.
