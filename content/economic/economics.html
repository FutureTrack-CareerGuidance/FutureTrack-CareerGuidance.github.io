ECONOMICS

OVERVIEW

What is Economics?

Economics is the study of how individuals, businesses, and governments allocate resources to meet their needs and wants. It is divided into two main branches: Microeconomics, which focuses on individual markets, consumers, and businesses; and Macroeconomics, which examines national and global economic systems, including inflation, GDP, unemployment, and government policies. In Australia, economists play a key role in policy-making, finance, banking, consulting, and data analysis. They help shape decisions on taxation, trade, interest rates, and economic growth.

What Will You Do?

  • Analyzing economic trends, financial markets, and policies
  • Conducting market research and forecasting economic conditions
  • Advising government agencies and businesses on financial planning
  • Working with data analytics, econometrics, and statistical modeling
  • Assessing the impact of monetary and fiscal policies

Step 1: Gain a Strong Economics Foundation

  • Master microeconomics, macroeconomics, and econometrics.
  • Learn to analyze GDP, inflation, and policy impacts.

Step 2: Develop Data & Analytical Skills

  • Learn Excel, SQL, R, or Python for data analysis.
  • Take courses on financial modeling and market research.

Step 3: Gain Practical Experience

‚úÖ Internships & Work Placements

  • Apply for internships in banks, consulting firms, and government agencies.
  • Participate in research projects and economic forecasting competitions.

Step 4: Network & Obtain Certifications

  • Join the Economic Society of Australia (ESA) for networking.
  • Consider CFA (Chartered Financial Analyst) for finance roles.

Step 5: Apply for Graduate Roles & Entry-Level Jobs

  • Target Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA), Deloitte, KPMG, and PwC.
  • Apply for government policy analyst roles and corporate strategy positions.

Current Market Snapshot

Current market size: Approximately $89.5 billion globally (2024).

Top 3 cities/regions with highest demand:

New York City, USA

Financial capital with major banks, investment firms, and consulting companies. High demand for economics graduates in finance, banking, and policy analysis. Strong economics departments at Columbia, NYU, and nearby Princeton.

London, UK

Global financial hub with concentration of economics jobs in banking, consulting, and government. Houses major economic institutions like the Bank of England and London School of Economics. Significant presence of international organizations requiring economic expertise.

Singapore

Asia's growing financial center with strong demand for economics professionals. Government-backed initiatives to become a global economic hub. High salaries and opportunities in both private and public sectors.
